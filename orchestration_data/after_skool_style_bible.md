### 1. Core Philosophy & Inner Qualia
The visual language of the After Skool animation channel is governed by a singular, foundational mission: to **“enhance profound ideas through art”**. Created almost entirely by San Francisco native Mark Wooding, the channel transcends traditional educational animation. It operates as a form of **psychological alchemy**, transmuting dense philosophical and scientific discourse into accessible visual narratives.

The overarching philosophy is rooted in the belief that external societal shifts begin with internal, individual transformations: “We cannot change the world, but we can change ourselves… and that will change the world.”

The unchanging “feeling” or inner qualia that drives every After Skool video is the pursuit of **illumination through cognitive dissonance and subsequent resolution**. Wooding structures his visual narratives to evoke a sense of **awe, humility, and deep introspection**. The experiential goal is not mere rote memorization, but the facilitation of a simulated internal journey — an “upside-down vortex leading to a unified vision of oneness.”

Wooding approaches animation as a **spiritual discipline**: art is a medium to channel “higher wisdom” and is akin to “channelling God through art.” The emotional qualia is characterized by a deliberate confrontation with darkness, followed by the revelation of light. “In order to see light you need the darkness. The more darkness there is, the more light you will see. Life is about finding the light in the darkness.”

Visually, this appears as complex problems drawn with dense, suffocating lines, only to be erased or morphed into expansive, harmonious symbols. The animations serve as a meditative focal point, reducing the viewer’s ego and instilling a sense of perfect harmony within a “great Universal whole.” This “selfish inward journey” is what resonates most with people in the outer world.

### 2. Topic & Idea Selection Process
Mark Wooding does not follow a rigid ideological agenda. He actively avoids being “attached to any ideology.”

The primary filter is the Japanese concept of **Ikigai** (reason for being). Every topic must fulfill these four overlapping criteria:

| Ikigai Component          | Application within After Skool's Topic Selection                                                                 | Anthropological & Creative Significance |
|---------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| What the Creator Loves    | Selections based on genuine, organic curiosity (history, geology, psychedelics, abstract philosophy)           | Ensures the grueling animation process is fueled by intrinsic motivation |
| What the Creator is Good At | Topics that lend themselves to complex visual metaphors, topological morphing, and continuous line drawing     | Avoids purely statistical or hyper-realistic subjects |
| What the World Needs      | Content that addresses mass psychosis, societal polarization, crisis of masculinity, or spiritual disconnection | Positions the channel as a therapeutic resource during cultural instability |
| What Can Be Monetized     | Balancing esoteric interests with algorithm-friendly hooks and recognizable speakers                           | Sustains reach with millions of subscribers |

Additional selection variables:
- High **philosophical resonance** (meaning of life, spirituality, psychedelics, history)
- The **unanswered question** hook that provokes immediate curiosity
- **Speaker energy and catharsis** — vocal cadence must translate into dynamic visual transformations
- Strategic balance between personal obsession and audience resonance (e.g., interspersing sacred geometry videos with more viral topics like Simon Sinek’s critique of millennials)

### 3. Research Methodology (The 4+ Layers)
Creating a single After Skool animation requires weeks of intensive preparation. The research is multi-layered and overlaps:

**Intellectual and Philosophical Research**  
Immerse in the core thesis. Read academic papers, study history, take geology tours, consult experts (e.g., for Bayesian networks or plant intelligence via Rupert Sheldrake).

**Speaker Vibe and Energy Matching**  
Repeatedly listen to the audio to map emotional topography, crescendos, pauses, and realization moments. Match visual tempo to the speaker’s psychological state.

**Visual Metaphor Layering & Symbolic Research**  
Translate abstract ideas using:
- Sacred Geometry and Mysticism (Seed of Life, psychedelic patterns)
- Archetypes and Alchemy (Jungian synchronicity, collective unconscious)
- Modern Dystopian Symbolism (corporate logos turned into monolithic doomsday metaphors)

**Artistic and Anatomical Reference Gathering**  
Study classical art, anatomical drawings, architectural blueprints, scientific illustrations. Memory alone is insufficient — references ensure stylized drawings remain grounded.

**Morphing Logic Research**  
Find physical and visual commonalities between disparate objects (topological continuity). Example: circular boundary of a pocket watch → human eye (shared center point and radiating lines).

### 4. Visual Language & Drawing Style Decisions

**Marker Stroke Quality, Line Weight, and Imperfections**  
Thick, bold, uniform dry-erase marker lines with slight organic wobbles, overlaps at corners, and variable opacity. Never sterile vector art.

**Color Palette**  
Strictly black lines on pure white background. Color used **extremely sparingly** as a cognitive spotlight (yellows, deep blues, gold for enlightenment or resolution). Negative or heavy topics remain monochromatic.

**Composition Rules on the Whiteboard**

| Composition Style     | Application and Cognitive Purpose                                      | Examples from After Skool |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Extreme Minimalism    | Deeply profound or existential statements; isolates focus             | Lone figure contemplating cosmos (Alan Watts) |
| High Density / Horror Vacui | Interconnected systems, societal chaos, complex networks             | Corporate life, mass psychosis, Bayesian networks |
| Symmetrical Mandalas  | Ultimate truths, natural harmony, sacred geometry                     | Story of Creation Through Sacred Geometry |

**Literal vs. Symbolic vs. Metaphorical Illustration**  
- **Literal**: Anchor in physical reality (exact brain, tree, historical figure)  
- **Symbolic**: Universally recognized icons (corporate logos = late-stage capitalism)  
- **Metaphorical**: Surreal blended imagery (dopamine stacking = tower of blocks collapsing inside skull; Purpose = dragon guarding treasure)

**Hidden Details and Easter Eggs**  
Esoteric symbols, recurring characters, subtle alchemical geometry — rewards multiple watches and creates an interconnected “After Skool Universe.”

### 5. Storyboarding & Morphing Logic
The hallmark is **continuous, fluid transformation** instead of cuts. Transitions rely on **associative chain and topological continuity** (shared geometric boundaries).

Techniques:
- Maintain outer boundary while internal details morph (coin → compass → steering wheel)
- Zoom infinitely into a small detail to transition to a new blank canvas
- Lines unravel into abstract geometry before coalescing into the new form

This reinforces the philosophical belief in a “unified vision of oneness.”

### 6. Pacing, Timing & Narrative Flow
Pacing is dictated by the speaker’s vocal cadence.

**The Rhythm of the Reveal**:
1. **Anticipation** — Begin drawing foundational shapes before the speaker reveals the concept
2. **The Drop** — Final identifying line completes exactly with the punchline (dopamine hit)
3. **The Pause** — Hold on completed image after heavy statements for absorption

Adapt entirely to pre-recorded narration: fast speech = broad strokes; long pauses = intricate background details.

### 7. Technical Execution (Hand-Drawn → Animated)
The “live drawing” is an illusion created digitally:

1. Hand-drawn digitally (Wacom Cintiq or iPad Pro + Photoshop/Procreate) on many layers
2. Imported into After Effects
3. **Mask Reveal Technique** — Alpha channel / track matte reveals lines in sequence
4. Pre-recorded sped-up human hand video overlaid and keyframed to follow the reveal path
5. Virtual camera movements (pans, zooms) for cinematic flow
